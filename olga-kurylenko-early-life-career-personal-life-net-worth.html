Olga Kurylenko Early Life, Career, Personal Life &amp; Net Worth

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Wednesday, June 5, 2024

Olga Kurylenko is a Ukrainian-French actress and former model born on November 14, 1979.

Originally from Berdyansk, she relocated to Paris at 16 to kickstart her modeling career.

Olga Kurylenko Early Life

Olga Kurylenko was born in Berdyansk, Ukrainian SSR, Soviet Union.

Her father, Konstantin, is of Ukrainian origin.

While her mother, Marina Alyabusheva, is an accomplished artist hailing from Irkutsk Oblast, Russia, with Russian and Belarusian heritage.

Her parents separated when she was three, and she was primarily brought up by her mother.

Kurylenko had minimal contact with her father, meeting him for the first time at the age of eight after their separation and later, at the age of thirteen.

Olga Kurylenko Career

Kurylenko moved from Berdyansk to Moscow at 15 and later to Paris at 16, signing with Madison modeling agency in 1996.

By 18, she graced the covers of Vogue and Elle and appeared in Madame Figaro and Marie Claire.

She became the face of brands like Bebe, Clarins, and Helena Rubinstein, and modeled for Roberto Cavalli and Kenzo.

Her music video appearances include French singer Faudel’s “Tellement Je T’aime” and Seal’s “Love’s Divine” in 2003.

Kurylenko’s film career began in 2004 with “The Ring Finger,” earning her a certificate of excellence at the 2006 Brooklyn International Film Festival.

She appeared in “Paris, je t’aime” in 2006 and became the face of Kenzo’s fragrance, Kenzo Amour.

After a decade in modeling, Kurylenko transitioned to acting in 2006.

Notable film roles include “Hitman” (2007), “Max Payne” (2008), and Bond girl Camille Montes in “Quantum of Solace” (2008).

She appeared on Maxim magazine covers and met Ukraine’s First Lady Kateryna Yushchenko in 2008.

Kurylenko’s film roles include Terrence Malick’s “To the Wonder” with Ben Affleck and Rachel McAdams, “Oblivion” with Tom Cruise, and the spy thriller “November Man.”

In 2014, she starred in “The Water Diviner” alongside Russell Crowe and played the headmistress in “Vampire Academy.”

Her later films include “Momentum,” “Mara,” and “The Bay of Silence.”

In 2017, she appeared in “The Death of Stalin” and starred in Terry Gilliam’s “The Man Who Killed Don Quixote” at Cannes.

She played Baroness Roxane de Giverny in “The Emperor of Paris” and a Russian spy in “Johnny English Strikes Again” (2018).

In 2021, she starred in “Sentinelle” and played Antonia Dreykov / Taskmaster in the MCU film “Black Widow.”

Kurylenko was cast in the comedy heist film “High Heat” alongside Don Johnson.

Olga Kurylenko Personal Life

Kurylenko obtained French citizenship in 2001 for practical travel benefits.

She married French fashion photographer Cedric van Mol in 2000 but divorced four years later.

In 2006, she married American mobile phone accessory entrepreneur Damian Gabrielle, with the marriage ending in divorce in 2007.

Kurylenko relocated to London in 2009.

She and her former partner, English actor and writer Max Benitz, have a son born in October 2015.
