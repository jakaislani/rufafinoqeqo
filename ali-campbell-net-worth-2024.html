Early Life and Career Beginnings

Ali Campbell was born in Birmingham, England, and grew up in a musical family. His father was a Scottish folk musician, which undoubtedly influenced Campbell’s own musical journey. He co-founded UB40 in 1978, and the band quickly gained popularity with their unique blend of reggae and pop.

Formation of UB40

The band’s name, UB40, was derived from a form issued to people claiming unemployment benefits at the time, reflecting the members’ own experiences and political views. Their music resonated with many, and they saw commercial success with hits like “Red Red Wine” and “Can’t Help Falling in Love.”

Ali Campbell’s Role in UB40
