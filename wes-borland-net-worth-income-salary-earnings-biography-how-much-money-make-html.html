Wesley Louden “Wes” Borland (born February 7, 1975) is an American rock musician and artist who is most known for his work with the band Wes Borland. Limp Bizkit, in which he now performs as guitarist and backing singer, as well as the industrial rock band Black Light Burns, are among his most well-known musical endeavours. During the late 1990s and early 2000s, when Limp Bizkit reached mainstream success, he earned widespread recognition. In 1998, he and his brother Scott created a rock band called Big Dumb Face, which became successful. After leaving Limp Bizkit in 2001, Borland embarked on a number of side projects, including Eat the Day and The Damned Well. He formed Black Light Burns after rejoining Limp Bizkit in 2004, and the band has produced three studio albums and a covers album to far. Following the release of their album The Unquestionable Truth (Part 1), Limp Bizkit announced that they would be taking a break (2005). However, they rejoined in 2009 and produced their sixth studio album, Gold Cobra, which was released in 20(2011). His extravagant visual look, which has featured face and body paint, masks, and costumes, has earned him a reputation for sonic experimentation as well as elaborate aesthetic appearances. He has produced record covers and artwork for several of his music ventures, as well as oil paintings, and he is a talented illustrator. A total of 37 votes were cast for Borland in Total Guitar’s poll of the Top 100 Guitarists of All-Time.
