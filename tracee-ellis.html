How rich is Tracee Ellis: What is His Net Worth

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Tuesday, September 3, 2024

Tracee Ellis Ross is a $16 million dollar net-worth American actress, comedian, model, filmmaker, and TV host. She is best known for starring in the television sitcoms “Girlfriends” and “Black-ish.” She’s also been in films including “Hanging Up,” “Daddy’s Little Girls,” and “The High Note.”

Aside from television and cinema, Ross is the owner of the hair-care business Pattern Beauty. Tracee is the daughter of singer Diana Ross and music manager Robert Ellis Silberstein.

Who is Tracee Ellis?

Tracee Ellis Ross was born Tracee Joy Silberstein in Los Angeles, California in 1972. She is the daughter of Motown singer and actress Diana Ross and music business manager Robert Ellis Silberstein, and her father is of Jewish descent. Ross has two sisters, Rhonda and Chudney, and two half-brothers, Evan and Ross. Katinka, Christoffer, and Leona are her three step-siblings from her mother’s remarriage to Arne Naess Jr.

Ross attended the Dalton School in Manhattan and Riverdale Country School in the Bronx as a child. She then went to the Institut Le Rosey in Rolle, Switzerland, for boarding education. Ross attended Brown University for her higher education, graduating in 1994 with a degree in theatre. She went on to work as a model and contributing fashion editor for New York and Mirabella magazines.

Ross made her television acting debut in 1998 when she appeared in the NBC film “Race Against Fear: A Moment of Truth.” She plays a former high school track star who is frightened to speak up about the abuse she received at the hands of her coach in the film. Ross’s hilarious performance on MTV’s “The Lyricist Lounge Show,” a hip-hop variety show that blended music with numerous skits and sketches, followed in 2000. The next year, Ross scored her breakout role on the UPN/CW sitcom “Girlfriends,” as Joan Carol Clayton, Esq., an accomplished and neurotic lawyer.

The sitcom, which followed the lives of a group of African-American women, aired for eight seasons and 172 episodes until 2008, garnering Ross two NAACP Image Awards for her portrayal. During this time, she also appeared in an episode of the sitcom “Second Time Around” and in the HBO television film “Life Support,” in which she co-starred with her brother Evan and Queen Latifah.

How much does Tracee Ellis make per year?

Tracee was paid $60,000 per episode throughout the first season. In the first season, her co-star and producer Anthony Anderson earned $100,000 for each episode. Tracee’s salary was upped to $200,000 per year after the syndication was secured. This equates to almost $4.4 million per season.

What investments does Tracee Ellis have?

Ross entered the $2.5 billion natural hair care market and introduced a hair care product specialized for Black hair after working on a brand pitch in 2008. It makes an effort to provide hair style products, treatments, and accessories for all types of curly hair.
Property Management

She also invests in real estate. Tracee resided in a Los Angeles home she bought for $910,000 for over a decade. She advertised this home for $2.3 million in March 2022.

She paid $780,000 for a studio apartment in New York City’s Tribeca neighborhood in 2007. In 2020, she sold this unit for $675,000.

How many Endorsement deals does Tracee Ellis have?

The actress and inventor of Pattern Haircare has been chosen as an ambassador for H&M’s collaboration with the group Buy From A Black Woman.

How many Philanthropy works has Tracee Ellis supported?

Ross’s support for charities includes Elton John AIDS Foundation, Susan G. Komen for the Cure, and United Negro College Fund.
